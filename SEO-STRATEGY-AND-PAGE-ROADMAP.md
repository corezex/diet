# DietFiniti SEO, AEO, GEO & AI-search Roadmap

**Prepared:** 22 July 2026  
**Primary commercial focus:** personalised weight-loss nutrition and online dietitian consultations  
**Confirmed service areas:** Mumbai, Thane, and online consultations across India  
**Brand entity:** DietFiniti, led by Dietitian Tejal

## Guardrails

- We will target qualified enquiries, not make ranking, health, or weight-loss guarantees.
- Medical nutrition content must be reviewed by Dietitian Tejal before publication and must not claim to cure, reverse, or replace medical treatment.
- Testimonials, ratings, client counts, credentials, awards, addresses, and prices will be used only where they are accurate, verifiable, and visible to users.
- We will not create doorway pages that repeat the same copy for every suburb or condition.

### Opportunity

DietFiniti can differentiate through a cleaner, more trustworthy proposition: practical Indian meals, personalised assessment, weekly adjustment/support, clear clinic and online access, and careful medical disclaimers. Competitor pages commonly overuse "best" claims and repeat keyword lists. We will be more useful, evidence-aware, and specific rather than copy that pattern.

## Page-by-page execution sequence

### Phase 0 — technical and entity foundation (completed in this workspace)
- sitemap, robots, canonical framework, central LocalBusiness/Organization schema, metadata baseline, broken internal-link fixes, disclaimer.

### Phase 1 — high-intent commercial pages
1. Homepage
2. Weight Loss service
3. Online Dietitian Consultation (new)
4. Contact / locations
5. Medical Weight Loss

For every page, implement: search intent, title/meta/canonical/Open Graph, one H1, semantic heading hierarchy, helpful direct-answer section, genuine FAQ, internal links, relevant image alt text, relevant JSON-LD, E-E-A-T review block, medical safety language, and conversion CTA.

### Phase 2 — specialised commercial pages
- Pregnancy, Weight Gain, Sports Nutrition, Bridal Diet, Services overview.

### Phase 3 — local authority and content
- Thane and Mumbai location information only where a real service location is confirmed.
- Publish reviewed articles that answer real client questions; do not mass-produce location variants.
- Add verified Google Business Profile, relevant health/business directories, and consistent NAP citations.

### Phase 4 — measurement and iteration
- Verify Google Search Console and Bing Webmaster Tools.
- Submit the sitemap.
- Track impressions, queries, indexed pages, Core Web Vitals, calls, WhatsApp leads, and contact-form conversion.
- Refresh pages using actual Search Console query data after sufficient impressions accrue.

## AEO / AI Overview approach

- Put a concise, direct answer immediately below each service-page H1.
- Answer comparison and process questions visibly on the page, not only in schema.
- Use named authors/reviewers, credentials, editorial dates, source citations for health statements, and transparent limitations.
- Use structured data only when it matches visible, factual page content.
- Build topical coverage and internal linking around practical Indian nutrition, not generic content volume.
